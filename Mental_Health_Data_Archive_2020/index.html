How does our Mental Health Data reflect us as a population? In the year 2020, humans experienced the harsh realities of the virus commonly known as Covid or Covid-19, the name signifying it's discovery in the year 2019. Mental Health has become an increasingly mainstream topic over the past decade with people being more vulnerable and open about their experiences with mental haelth through the use of social media and connecting with others. The mental health of the human population was put to the test in the past 3 years, especially in the year 2020. This was the year that many friends and family memebers became sick and lost their lives to the virus, people lost there jobs and were faced with unemployment, and many of us had to isolate and change our overall lives to accomodate this deadly virus.

Using the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Data Archive (SAMHA), we are able to take a look at client-level data and analyze how different populations were represented with having different diagnosis during the year 2020. The filters can be used to select a state, race, age, and gender to better understand how different marginalized groups compare when being represented in the data. Some populations have more data provided than others, that also is an indication of who is with or without access to services. We must also take into account that mental health is still stigmatized in some ways within different populations which may limit our search and findings. I am also planning to add 2019 data as another year of data if I can get the filter engine to sync properly.
